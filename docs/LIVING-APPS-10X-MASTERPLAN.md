# Living Apps ŌĆö the Agentic App becomes a place an agent *lives*, not just a tool it *uses*

> **Status:** Proposed ┬Ę 2026-06-26
> **Extends (does not fork):** `AGENTIC-APPS-10X-MASTERPLAN.md` (the App primitive), `OMNICHANNEL-ORCHESTRATOR-10X.md` (the channelŌåÆturn loop), `GENUI-RENAISSANCE-MASTERPLAN.md` (the expressive interface layer). This plan does not rebuild any of them ŌĆö it **joins** them.
> **One line:** *An Agentic App is a living digital worker you instantiate: it perceives across many channels, thinks with a memory and skills that compound, acts, expresses itself as a calm operator console, and gets measurably better over time ŌĆö staffed by a team that is born with it.*

---

## 0. The realization

Agentis already builds **automations** well: something happens, the App's workflow runs, the datastore updates, a surface shows it. But the agent era's other half is the agent that is simply **present** ŌĆö an online sales attendant who greets every visitor, qualifies them, remembers the objection from three days ago, follows up Thursday as promised, and escalates to a human when the deal is real.

Agentis already *has* that resident agent. **It just doesn't live in the App.** It lives on a parallel spine the App cannot see. The question that opened this plan ŌĆö *"is the App the right home for an online agent, or does the agent merely use the App as a tool?"* ŌĆö has an honest answer in the code: today it's the latter, by accident. This masterplan makes the App the home.

---

## 1. The gap, grounded

Agentis has **two disjoint spines** that never meet.

**Spine A ŌĆö the resident conversational agent** (real, working, App-blind):
```
channel_connections (agentId ŌĆö NOT appId)         schema.ts:1015
   ŌåÆ ChannelBridge.handleInbound
   ŌåÆ ChannelTurnDispatcher.dispatch()              channelTurnDispatcher.ts
        ŌåÆ resolve the connection's bound agent
        ŌåÆ ChatSessionExecutor.turn()               (the agent actually thinks)
        ŌåÆ conversations (agentId, channelConnectionId)  schema.ts:810
        ŌåÆ deliver the reply to the channel
```
A genuine persistent agent ŌĆö long-lived threads, a 20-message window (`HISTORY_LIMIT`), Brain memory, cross-channel identity recall, sticky permission mode. **It has no concept of an App:** `channelTurnDispatcher.ts` has zero `appId` references; a channel binds to an *agent*, a conversation is owned by an *agent*. Neither can point at an App.

**Spine B ŌĆö the Agentic App** (the primitive, conversation-blind): `apps ŌåÆ workflows + collections + surfaces + app_members + ownerAgentId` ([schema.ts:2653](../packages/db/src/sqlite/schema.ts)). `routes/apps.ts` has zero `conversation` references. The App cannot see, own, or render the live relationship.

**The tell: the UI already dreams of the bridge.** The GenUI grammar already ships the conversational vocabulary ŌĆö `Inbox`, `ChatThread`, `ConversationThread`, `AgentConsole`, `ActivityStream` are real, renderable nodes. But every one binds to **App datastore rows** via `useBoundRows` ([ViewRenderer.tsx:1552](../apps/web/src/components/apps/ViewRenderer.tsx)), and `send` is a datastore/tool action. An App's "Inbox" today is a **photograph** of conversations copied into a collection ŌĆö not the live thread, and sending from it runs no real turn.

> The interface vocabulary is ahead of the runtime. The grammar imagines a living conversational app; the substrate behind it is a still life. This plan closes exactly that distance ŌĆö and then goes past it.

## 2. The model: an App is a composition, not a mode

An App is **not** typed `triggered | resident`. That binary is a false ceiling ŌĆö it would throw away the plural activation Agentis already has and scatter `if (resident) ŌĆ”` through the codebase. An App is a **living service composed from orthogonal dials**, each independent and mixable:

- **Senses (activation)** ŌĆö how it wakes: `manual | cron | webhook | persistent_listener` ([workflow.ts:22](../packages/core/src/schemas/workflow.ts)), a channel message, a data change, another agent, or its own standing goal. Already six-plus forms, built (`ListenerRuntime`, `scheduler`, `broadcastDispatcher`, `channelTurnDispatcher`, `TriggerRuntime`, `ActiveWorkflowRegistry`).
- **Persistence** ŌĆö one-shot ŌåÆ session ŌåÆ long relationship ŌåÆ perpetual-standing.
- **Counterparts** ŌĆö one human ┬Ę many humans ┬Ę systems/data ┬Ę other agents ┬Ę the physical world ┬Ę itself.
- **Surfaces** ŌĆö chat ┬Ę operator console ┬Ę voice ┬Ę email ┬Ę public web ┬Ę API ┬Ę embed.
- **Staff** ŌĆö a single agent ┬Ę a cast ┬Ę a swarm ┬Ę a hierarchy.

"Automation" and "resident attendant" are just two points in this space, and most real apps span several at once. New application shapes are **new compositions of primitives that already exist**, never new modes or new code:

| Shape | Senses | Persistence | Counterpart | Surface |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Automation | event/webhook | one-shot | systems | log |
| Resident attendant (sales/support) | channel + schedule | standing | one human | console + chat |
| Monitor / watchdog | data stream + listener | standing | systems | console + alerts |
| Researcher / creator | own goal + schedule | standing | itself + web | artifacts |
| Broadcaster / community | inbound + schedule | standing | many humans | broadcast + inbox |
| Co-pilot | human acts on a surface | session | one human | embedded surface |
| Voice line / field agent | voice/webhook | sessionŌåÆstanding | human / world | voice |
| Agent-to-agent service | another agent's call | one-shotŌåÆstanding | other agents | API |

**The invariant:** there is **no `app.mode` flag anywhere.** The resident console is simply an App whose senses include a channel and whose persistence is standing. Generality and minimalism agree here ŌĆö the composable model has no branch, so it is both more open and more minimal. *If we ever feel tempted to write `app.mode`, that is the signal we took the wrong turn.*

**What we build first.** The **resident-conversational composition** ŌĆö because it is the hardest preset (it forces the channelŌåöApp spine, multi-party threads, the live surface) and the one needed now. We build it as the *first instance of the general model*, not as one of two types.

**Constraints we hold throughout:** additive and nullable (an App with no channels behaves exactly as today); one render path (edit = live = operator = public); we extend `ChannelTurnDispatcher` / `conversations` / `view.ts` / `ViewRenderer` / `apps`, never fork a parallel "conversation app" subsystem (`feedback_no_duplication`); and the agent does the wiring ŌĆö no operator settings panel where a default will do (`feedback_no_friction_defaults`).

## 3. The organism ŌĆö perceive ┬Ę think ┬Ę act ┬Ę express ┬Ę grow

The deepest reframe is what an App *is*. Today Agentis assembles a service from **static parts**: a UI artifact + a prompt + a connection. The bolder model treats it as **one organism** ŌĆö the App is the **body**, Brain + Abilities the **mind**, the surface the **face**, channels the **senses**, and graduation the capacity to **grow**. That is the difference between "AI-powered CRM apps" and *the substrate where you instantiate a digital worker that gets measurably better* ŌĆö the actual #1-platform claim. Four design principles follow.

### 3.1 The interface is *performed*, not authored
The surface is a **live projection of the agent's attention**, not a frozen `app_surfaces` row. The `ViewNode` grammar stays (it's good; `CodeSurface` is the long-tail hatch) but becomes a **streaming medium**: a stable, calm frame (inbox ┬Ę active thread) hosts **agent-composed regions** ŌĆö when 40 threads share a churn signal, the agent renders a "churn risk" panel *unprompted* and it appears. The UI becomes an output channel of cognition.
*DNA guardrail (alive, not flickering):* stable skeleton + agent-zones only; every agent-composed region is **explainable** ("added because 12 deals stalled at 'pricing'") and the operator can **pin / freeze** it.

### 3.2 The specialist is *grown*, not configured ŌĆö and abilities are part of the agent
An agent's competence is **persona + its pinned abilities (always composed into context) + context-selected abilities + brain-recalled lessons** ŌĆö assembled every turn, never a frozen prompt.
- **Pinned abilities are identity.** Today abilities fire **only on an explicit `/slash`** ([chatSessionExecutor.ts:267](../apps/api/src/services/chatSessionExecutor.ts)) even though they can already be *pinned to an agent*. A pinned ability must be composed into the operating context every turn, like the persona; slash becomes a manual override, not the only trigger.
- **Auto-selected abilities** (the Ability Composer, the real remaining gap ŌĆö deferred in `project_abilities_10x`): mid-conversation the agent reaches for its "price-objection" ability because the customer raised price, composed by relevance.
- **A learning loop:** every won/lost/abandoned conversation deposits a **graded lesson**; recurring winning patterns **graduate into new abilities** (reuse the self-eval + graduation-by-reuse machinery ŌĆö `project_pacer_feynman`). The operator can *watch the skill grow*.
- **Where competence lives:** facts & relationships ŌåÆ Brain; reusable how-to-operate ŌåÆ Abilities (a first-class facet of the agent); non-negotiable doctrine ŌåÆ the manual. *Memory is already real:* recall is semantic by default (bundled multilingual ONNX `Xenova/multilingual-e5-small`, **no lexical fallback**, model-id-checked ŌĆö [embeddingProvider.ts:220](../apps/api/src/services/embeddingProvider.ts), [sharedIntelligence.ts:348](../apps/api/src/services/sharedIntelligence.ts)). The residual is not "can it remember" but "does the resident loop **feed and scope** that memory" (per-conversation/contact lessons + contact-scoped recall ŌĆö G11).

### 3.3 A resident App is staffed by a *cast*, not one agent
A desk is a team ŌĆö greeter, qualifier, objection-handler, closer, scheduler ŌĆö each a composable specialist you can hire, clone, train, and swap, **handing off inside one thread** (which is *why* multi-party threads, G1, are load-bearing). The org spine exists (`project_subdomains_specialist_responsibility`); it isn't yet composed into a living team that improves part-by-part.

### 3.4 Specialists are normal agents; what's missing is *creation*
A specialist is a **normal Agentis agent**, reusable across apps and any task ŌĆö no app-imprisonment, no residence binding. The workspace talent pool and `SpecialistDemandRouter` stay. `app_members` is already a many-to-many join, so one agent already *can* serve many apps. The failure is at birth: `AppStore.create` ([appStore.ts:95](../packages/app/src/appStore.ts)) sets `ownerAgentId: null` and inserts **zero** `app_members`; the `app.create` tool passes no staff. **An App is born unowned and empty of people**, even though `SpecialistAgentService`, `specialist_loadout` (roleŌåÆabilities), and the membership join all already exist. The fix is one new step ŌĆö *birth-staff-at-creation* (┬¦4.6) ŌĆö not a new ontology. Everything else org-side (managers own subdomains; an app's own staff answers for app work first) is **doctrine + a precedence tweak**, never a schema constraint.

## 4. Architecture

### 4.1 Ownership ŌĆö the App adopts channels and conversations *(additive, nullable)*
- `channel_connections.app_id ŌåÆ apps.id (set null)` ŌĆö a channel can belong to an App.
- `conversations.app_id ŌåÆ apps.id (set null)` ŌĆö a thread can belong to an App; backfilled from the connection on inbound.
- New table `app_contacts` ŌĆö the **relationship** entity the App is missing: one row per person, unifying their conversations across channels (keyed off `channel_identities`). Fields: `appId, displayName, primaryChannel, handles[], stage, goal, lastTouchAt, nextTouchAt, dataJson`. This is what turns a pile of threads into a pipeline.

`channel_connections.app_id` makes the App the unit you **connect, deploy, pause, and hand off** ŌĆö "connect this WhatsApp line to the Sales App" is the mental model.

### 4.2 The resident turn ŌĆö context injection, not a new engine
When `connection.appId` is set, `ChannelTurnDispatcher` runs the *same* turn it already runs, with an **App-scoped context** added: the agent's tool catalog gains the App's datastore (`agentis.appData.*`), its actions, and its operating doctrine ("you are the resident agent of *Acme Sales*; persist what you learn to `contacts`/`deals`; your goal for this contact isŌĆ”"). The turn writes to the datastore as durable working memory (`project_agentic_apps_data_loop`), and the reply streams to **both** the channel and the App's console via the realtime bus the dispatcher already publishes on. *No new turn engine ŌĆö "run the turn I already run, but in App context, and broadcast it to the console."*

### 4.3 Live conversational nodes *(additive grammar)*
`Inbox`, `ChatThread`, `ConversationThread` gain an optional `source`:
- `source:'collection'` ŌĆö today's behavior, unchanged (datastore rows).
- `source:'conversations'` ŌĆö binds to the **real** `conversations`/`messages` spine, scoped to the App; filters by channel/contact/stage/unread; `send` runs a **real agent turn** (or posts an operator message) through the dispatcher.
The renderer gets `useLiveConversations(appId, filter)` (parallel to `useBoundRows`). The agent authoring the surface picks the source; the operator never configures it.

### 4.4 The datastore as shared working memory
Resident agent and surfaces read/write the same typed collections: the agent learns "budget $40k, decision Q3" ŌåÆ writes `contacts`/`deals` ŌåÆ the pipeline board updates live ŌåÆ a follow-up workflow reads `nextTouchAt`. One memory, three readers ŌĆö the data loop now spanning conversation *and* automation.

### 4.5 Proactivity ŌĆö follow-ups as first-class
A resident agent must **initiate**. `app_contacts.nextTouchAt` + the existing `SchedulerService.sweepDue` (`project_live_workspace_steps_10x`) ŌåÆ at the due time, dispatch a turn into the contact's conversation ("follow up as promised"); the agent composes and sends. The machinery exists ŌĆö it just needs binding to a contact instead of a workflow node.

### 4.6 Birth-staff-at-creation
The App creation pipeline gains a **`staff` step**: for each role the app needs, **reuse a fitting workspace agent** if one exists, else **materialize a new specialist** via `SpecialistAgentService`; **pin its abilities** from `specialist_loadout`; **seat it in `app_members`** (one `operator`, the rest `worker`); set `apps.ownerAgentId`. The cast is **derived from the app archetype** (Sales ŌåÆ greeter/qualifier/closer; analytics ŌåÆ one operator-analyst) and editable after ŌĆö zero-config. A born specialist arrives with **competence** (composed abilities, ┬¦3.2), not merely an extension list. A small precedence tweak lets `resolveResponsibleSpecialist` consult `app_members` first for app work; the org canvas relabels managers as Domain/Subdomain owners and apps as where specialists work ŌĆö no migration, `domains.managerId` stays permissive.

## 5. The creation doctrine ŌĆö the building agent must *know* it can do all this

None of the above matters if the agent that builds apps doesn't know these shapes exist. App-building knowledge lives in `orchestratorPrompt.ts` (`PLATFORM_KNOWLEDGE`, the numbered build steps in `PLATFORM_ARCHITECTURE_KNOWLEDGE`, `ORCHESTRATOR_BEHAVIOR_RULES`) and the imported `WORKFLOW_DESIGN_DOCTRINE` ŌĆö the same place that already teaches "an App = {identity, surfaces, logic, data, agents, policy}" and "2b. CLOSE THE DATA LOOP." Today that doctrine describes only the **automation** shape and says nothing about senses, resident apps, staffing, or the possibility space. We extend it (and mirror it into `agentOperatingManual.ts` so resident agents carry the same self-knowledge, and into the creation pipeline like `WORKFLOW_DESIGN_DOCTRINE`):

- **An App can be any composition (┬¦2), not just a triggered workflow.** Teach the dials: choose the senses (channel / schedule / listener / event / self-goal), the persistence, the counterpart, the surfaces. Give worked recipes ŌĆö "a 24/7 attendant," "a monitor," "a broadcaster" ŌĆö so the agent reaches past the automation default.
- **Staff the App at creation (┬¦4.6).** When building an App that operates continuously or talks to people, **assemble its team**: reuse or create specialists, pin their abilities, seat them, set the owner ŌĆö *with abilities, not just extensions*. An app that operates a relationship but has no resident staff is incomplete, exactly as "logic with no interface/data" is already called incomplete.
- **Close the *relationship* loop, not only the data loop.** The existing "persist results to the collection the UI binds to" doctrine generalizes: a resident agent persists what it learns to `app_contacts`/`conversations`, sets `nextTouchAt`, and renders the live `source:'conversations'` console ŌĆö so the interface reflects the living relationship, not a snapshot.
- **Compose abilities into the agents you create.** When authoring a specialist for an App, draw its loadout from `specialist_loadout` / the ability library and pin it ŌĆö the agent is born competent.
- **Still one App, edited in place.** All current anti-duplication doctrine holds (find-and-edit the existing App; `app.create` is idempotent) ŌĆö staffing and senses are added to the *same* App, never a renamed clone.

**Net:** the agent should treat "what kind of living thing should this App be, and who should staff it?" as part of *every* build ŌĆö the way it already treats "what's the data model and does the loop close?"

## 7. Deeper gaps ŌĆö what the current design *cannot* express

Real architectural ceilings, ranked by how badly an agentic solution needs them.

- **G1 ┬Ę Multi-party threads.** `conversations.agentId` is **notNull and singular** ([schema.ts:819](../packages/db/src/sqlite/schema.ts)). A real desk needs customer + resident agent + escalation specialist + human in **one** thread. **Fix:** a `conversation_participants` join, `agentId` demoted to "primary." *The single highest-leverage change.*
- **G2 ┬Ę Durable turns at scale.** The dispatcher is fire-and-forget, in-process (`void this.#executeTurn`) ŌĆö no worker pool, no backpressure, no resumption on restart (`project_specialists_seam` deferred turn-durability). **Fix:** a durable turn queue (`DURABLE-TASK-SPINE-10X`) with at-least-once delivery + per-App concurrency.
- **G3 ┬Ę The relationship has no home.** Threads are unified by channel handle, never by person. **Fix:** `app_contacts` (┬¦4.1) with cross-channel identity merge.
- **G4 ┬Ę Long-horizon memory.** `HISTORY_LIMIT=20` + recall, but no rolling per-conversation summary ŌĆö the agent forgets the middle of a month-long deal. **Fix:** per-conversation summarization (Brain formation machinery exists ŌĆö `project_brain_memory_formation` ŌĆö just unbound to live threads).
- **G5 ┬Ę Agent-initiated outreach.** Everything routes from an inbound webhook; nothing lets an agent open a thread proactively. **Fix:** an outbound dispatch path symmetric to the inbound one (the bridge already *delivers*; what's missing is the agent *initiating*).
- **G6 ┬Ę Voice / real-time media.** Text webhooks only; no streaming-audio channel kind or barge-in turn model. **Fix:** a new channel tier ŌĆö large; own masterplan, named now so the channel abstraction doesn't exclude it.
- **G7 ┬Ę Outbound safety envelope.** No per-App rate limit, outbound budget, claim-allowlist, or "never promise X" beyond the generic confirm gate. A commercial agent can't ship without it. **Fix:** per-App outbound policy in `apps.policyJson` (the column exists; enforcement doesn't).
- **G8 ┬Ę No rehearsal.** You can test workflows, not a multi-turn conversation against the resident agent. **Fix:** a conversation simulator (a synthetic counterpart driving scored scenarios).
- **G9 ┬Ę Co-presence.** Ō£ģ **SHIPPED (presence + agent-thinking/operator-typing)** ŌĆö ephemeral presence roster (`AppPresenceService`, in-memory + heartbeat/TTL) + agent-activity (`thinking`/`typing`/`idle`) over the EXISTING realtime bus (`APP_PRESENCE_UPDATED`/`APP_AGENT_ACTIVITY`, dual-published app+workspace); a calm presence row + "agent is thinking/typingŌĆ”" line in the live console. *Deferred:* customer-typing (no inbound-typing webhook) + multi-operator cursor-level co-editing.
- **G10 ┬Ę The specialist can't learn to operate, and its own abilities are dormant.** Static operating manual ([agentOperatingManual.ts:16](../apps/api/src/services/agentOperatingManual.ts)); abilities slash-gated ([chatSessionExecutor.ts:267](../apps/api/src/services/chatSessionExecutor.ts)). **Fix:** pinned abilities as always-on identity + Ability Composer + the graduation loop (┬¦3.2).
- **G11 ┬Ę The mind remembers, but the resident loop doesn't feed or scope it.** Recall is semantic and real ([embeddingProvider.ts:220](../apps/api/src/services/embeddingProvider.ts)) ŌĆö the residual is per-conversation/contact lessons aren't written from threads and recall isn't contact-scoped. **Fix:** bind brain-formation to the live thread + scoped recall. A wiring job over working machinery.
- **G12 ┬Ę The UI is a stored artifact, not a performed projection.** **Fix:** an agent-driven `SURFACE_RENDER` region channel over the existing realtime seam (┬¦3.1).
- **G13 ┬Ę Apps are born empty of people.** `AppStore.create` staffs nothing despite `app_members` + `SpecialistAgentService` + `specialist_loadout` existing. **Fix:** birth-staff-at-creation (┬¦4.6) ŌĆö the narrowest, highest-leverage gap in the org story.

> G1, G2, G3 are **load-bearing** ŌĆö without them "resident App" is a demo. G4ŌĆōG13 are the difference between a product and *the #1 platform of the agent era*.

## 8. Phasing

> **Spine track** (own + render the living relationship) and **Mind track** (make the specialist grown) run in parallel. The Mind track is unblocked ŌĆö semantic brain recall already ships.

- **Phase 0 ŌĆö App owns the relationship.** `app_id` on `channel_connections` + `conversations`; App-scoped turn context; data loop spans conversation. *ŌåÆ a WhatsApp line on an App answers in App context and persists.*
- **Phase R ŌĆö Birth-staff-at-creation (G13).** The `staff` step (reuse-or-materialize agents, pin `specialist_loadout` abilities, seat in `app_members`, set owner) + precedence tweak + org-canvas relabel + the creation doctrine (┬¦5). *Small, self-contained, high-leverage ŌĆö proves ┬¦3.2/┬¦3.3/┬¦3.4 at once.* *ŌåÆ every new App is born owned and staffed, agents arriving competent.*
- **Phase 1 ŌĆö Live conversational surfaces.** `source:'conversations'`; `useLiveConversations`; the `relationship` archetype. *ŌåÆ open the App, watch live threads stream in.*
- **Phase 2 ŌĆö The human moment (needs G1).** `conversation_participants`; take-over/hand-back; needs-you flags; in-thread approval. *ŌåÆ seamless human step-in.*
- **Phase 3 ŌĆö Relationship + proactivity.** `app_contacts`; identity merge; `nextTouchAt` ŌåÆ proactive follow-up; pipeline board. *ŌåÆ a desk that remembers and follows up.*
- **Phase M1 ŌĆö Abilities become part of the agent (G10).** *Step 1:* pinned abilities composed every turn (un-gate `/slash`). *Step 2:* the Ability Composer (auto-select by relevance). *ŌåÆ the agent applies its own skills unbidden.*
- **Phase M2 ŌĆö The learning loop (G10).** Won/lost ŌåÆ graded lesson ŌåÆ graduated ability; a "learned this week" panel. *ŌåÆ measurably better over time, visibly.*
- **Phase 4 ŌĆö Conversational construction, finished.** Live `SurfacePatch` dialogue as the primary authoring path.
- **Phase M3 ŌĆö Performed surfaces + in-thread handoff (G12, G1).** Agent-driven `SURFACE_RENDER` regions (stable frame + pinnable); the cast hands off in one thread. *ŌåÆ the console composes itself; the team hands off seamlessly.*
- **Phase 5 ŌĆö Durability + safety + rehearsal (G2, G7, G8).** Durable turn queue + per-App concurrency; outbound policy; conversation simulator. *ŌåÆ ships to real money, 24/7.*

**Deferred / own masterplans:** voice channels (G6) shipped a non-streaming FOUNDATION (webhook-transcription voice as just-another-channel) ŌĆö real-time streaming/barge-in/STT remains its own masterplan; long-horizon summarization binding (G4). Co-presence (G9) shipped its core (presence + agent-thinking/operator-typing); only customer-typing + multi-operator cursor-level co-editing remain.

## 9. Non-goals
- No new chat *engine* ŌĆö the resident composition is context injection over `ChatSessionExecutor`/`ChannelTurnDispatcher`.
- No `app.mode` flag, ever ŌĆö App shape is a composition of dials (┬¦2).
- No parallel "conversation app" entity ŌĆö Apps absorb the spine; we don't fork it.
- No agent imprisonment ŌĆö specialists stay normal, reusable agents; no `home_app_id`.
- No operator-facing wiring config ŌĆö the agent authors; defaults derive.
- Multi-tenant authz stays out of scope (OSS single-operator ŌĆö `project_masterplan_2026_06_audit`).

## 10. North star
> An Agentic App is not a tool an agent uses ŌĆö it is a **living digital worker you instantiate, born with its team, and it grows.** Give it senses and it wakes: it perceives across every channel, thinks with a memory and skills that compound, acts through workflows and tools, expresses itself as a calm console that composes around what it's seeing, and gets measurably better every week ŌĆö as a cast, not a script. You watch, direct, and approve; it does the work, the wiring, and the learning. Fix the App's *ownership and lifecycle* ŌĆö let the agent live there, be staffed there, and learn there ŌĆö and the App becomes the home an online agent deserves, and Agentis becomes the substrate the agent era is built on.
